Many students have only a vague notion of what is required for them to obtain a job in their intended career field, and even more think they will land a position in an organization without relevant industry experience. This is naïve. If you don't prepare yourself for work in your desired career until after you graduate, you'll be too late. The labor market is very competitive. There are not nearly enough jobs for the number of people graduating each year with bachelor's degrees. Indeed, numerous surveys demonstrate that anywhere between 48-53% of new college and university graduates are unemployed or underemployed in jobs that do not require a bachelor's degree a year after graduation. Furthermore, the average amount of student loan debt is over $32,000.

This project is intended to help you discover what you need to do to prepare yourself for the labor market--before you graduate. I hope you will find it informative and useful to you as you move closer to graduation.
